# Replacing pnpm to bun?
**Date: 18/4/2026**

---

I like fast and reliable. That's why I use `pnpm` for my package manager. It's fast, it's low on disk usage, and it's still the same as `npm`. When I heard `bun` a couple hours ago, I was intruged. Could this replace my current workflow for `pnpm`? Well, I went ahead and gave it a shot.

When I tried it, it was actually pretty cool. Hot reloading on basic `index.html`? Sounds awesome. And it's faster than vite? Sure. I gave it a couple of hours to use it and it's pretty fun. But when it comes to installing packages. It takes such a long time. Plus, I'm pretty sure `bun` is just like `npm` when it comes to installing packages. Takes up a lot of space, doesn't reuse packages, and overall just takes up a lot of storage.

I really wanted to like `bun`, but honestly, I think I'd stick with `pnpm` for now. But probably in the future, I'll use `bun` again. Who knows?
